Analytics
Analytics
provides you with high-level overview of the monitored systems and alerts. At a single glance you can learn what’s going on and how all of your SAP systems perform. The panel is a powerful tool for monitoring and analysis; actually it’s a highly-functional real-time BI dashboard.
The upper toolbar contains buttons for managing filters and properties:
Click on the
<Filters>
button to open the side panel for selecting filter values:
Click on the button at the right side of a filter selector to open a popover for selecting the values:
Once you are done with selecting the filters’ values click
<G0>
button to apply the filters or
<Reset>
button to clear the selection:
Properties
panel provides you with detailed information about Alerts, Recipients and other data displayed in the central frame. From here you can also drill down to
Wizard – Basic Settings
to perform changes. Click
<Properties>
button to display the side panel:
Click
<Export Analytics to
Excel>
button to export the data to Excel:
Source system
toolbar displays the information about the monitored systems: their names, status (Active/Inactive) and number of caught events for each of them:
The central part of the central frame contains high-level KPIs. The upper row displays alert-related data in comparison with the previous period’s values. Actual figure is displayed at the right side, previous figure for comparison is displayed at the left side. Color and direction of the arrows indicate whether the actual figure increased (red), decreased (green) or didn’t change (grey) since the previous period:
The second row displays only actual data about notifications, users and recipients. From each panel here you can drill down to detailed information displayed at the

The charts at the lower part of the central frame are used for comparative analysis of today’s data against the period’s average. They help to quickly figure out whether there is deviation in Alert quantity and/or alert run time against average values. Big deviations mean that the monitored systems suffer from severe problems.
The left chart provides info about monitored events’ summary, the right one – about average Alert run time. The data is displayed at the level of separate Alert Instances:
By default the charts show only daily data. Click on the chart titles to switch on/off weekly and daily data. Color of the chart titles correspond with the color of the chart lines:
